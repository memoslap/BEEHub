Semantic retrieval task requiring covert generation of an action verb in response to visually presented nouns.

Background

Verb generation reliably activates left inferior frontal gyrus (Broca's area), left posterior temporal cortex, and anterior cingulate cortex, reflecting the demand for semantic selection among competing alternatives. The task shows robust individual differences in latency and is sensitive to frontal lobe lesions, ageing, and language disorders. Because the generate–repeat contrast isolates top-down semantic retrieval, it is widely used as a functional localiser and as an index of semantic network efficiency.
