Virtual environment navigation task assessing allocentric landmark learning and flexible route planning.

Background

Spatial navigation is a fundamental cognitive capacity supported by the hippocampus, entorhinal cortex, and retrosplenial cortex. Place cells and grid cells provide the neural substrate for cognitive map formation, and individual differences in navigation ability correlate with hippocampal grey matter volume and functional connectivity. SPATNAV uses a standardised virtual environment to enable cross-laboratory comparability and has been used to study age-related navigation decline, sex differences in navigation strategy, and the effects of stress on spatial memory.
