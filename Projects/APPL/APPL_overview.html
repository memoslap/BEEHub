Language learning task probing the acquisition of novel word–meaning associations across repeated exposures.

Background

Word learning in adulthood involves not only phonological encoding but also the rapid formation of arbitrary form–meaning links. The APPL task operationalises this by presenting pseudowords that must be associated with visual referents over a series of study–test cycles. Repeated exposure across two sessions (with a multi-day interval) allows measurement of consolidation and test-retest reliability of the learning index. The task is sensitive to individual differences in language aptitude and is used as a translational model of second-language acquisition in healthy and clinical populations.
